# Experimental Design — "The Listening Gap" (working title)

**Do LLMs know what "warm" sounds like? Probing parametric audio-engineering knowledge
against crowdsourced human gold, and testing whether retrieval grounding closes the gap.**

## 1. Research questions

- **RQ1 (knowledge probe):** How accurately do LLMs map semantic audio descriptors
  (e.g. "warm", "bright", "muddy") to 5-band parametric EQ curves, relative to
  crowdsourced human gold data (SAFE-DB)?
- **RQ2 (access vs. capability):** Does retrieval grounding — interpolation over
  human-derived per-descriptor centroids — outperform zero-shot LLM generation?
- **RQ3 (does generation add value):** Does a hybrid RAG arm (LLM generating *with*
  retrieved human exemplars in context) outperform both pure retrieval and pure
  generation?
- **RQ4 (cross-family divergence):** Do EQ priors differ systematically across LLM
  families, or do all families share the same learned mapping?

RQ2 is the Access-Not-Capability framing ported to audio: if the LLM fails zero-shot
but succeeds with retrieved exemplars, the deficit is access, not capability.

## 2. Hypotheses (directional where justified; frozen at design lock)

- **H1:** Zero-shot LLM curves (Arm B) fall measurably outside the human curve
  distribution per descriptor, quantified by Human-Relative Percentile (§6.3) > 75
  on the primary spectral metric.
- **H2:** The hybrid arm (C) scores closer to the human distribution than zero-shot
  (B). Paired, per-descriptor.
- **H3 (decomposition):** LLM errors are predominantly *magnitude* errors, not
  *direction* errors — models know "warm" boosts lows and rolls off highs, but
  miscalibrate by how much. Tested by separating sign-agreement (derived binary,
  reported alongside) from continuous gain-magnitude error. This is the
  granularity-signature decomposition: the interesting failure lives in the
  continuous scale, not the binary gate.
- **H4:** At least one model-family pair differs significantly on the primary metric
  distribution (two-sided; no direction prior).
- **H5 (distribution coverage):** On distribution-matching metrics (§6.4), sampled
  LLM curve *clouds* can outperform the centroid arm, because a centroid is a point
  mass with zero variance and human "warm" is a distribution. (This keeps Arm A
  honest — see §7.1.)
- **H6 (grounding beats scale):** A 1–3B model fine-tuned on TRAIN human curves
  (Arm D) achieves better HRP than frontier zero-shot models (Arm B) that are
  ~2 orders of magnitude larger — i.e., the deficit is access to human gold, not
  model capability/scale. Conditional on the Arm D viability gate (§4).
- **H7 (exploratory, no direction prior):** Arm E (embedding→MLP) vs. Arm D
  (fine-tuned LLM) — does language-model machinery add anything over a tiny
  regressor for this mapping? Either outcome is reportable.
- **H8 (factorial interaction):** RAFT (Arm F) outperforms both fine-tuning
  alone (D) and RAG alone (C) — training the model to *use* retrieved human
  curves beats either grounding mechanism in isolation. Conditional on the
  Arm D/F viability gate.

## 3. Dataset

**SAFE-DB Equaliser user data** (Stables, Enderby, De Man, Fazekas & Reiss, ISMIR
2014). Crowdsourced entries from the SAFE VST plugin: each row = one human audio
engineer's 5-band parametric EQ setting labeled with a free-text semantic descriptor,
plus metadata (genre, instrument, location, experience).

**Parameter space (13 dims per entry):**

| Band | Params |
|---|---|
| Low shelf (LSC) | gain (dB), freq (Hz) |
| Band 1 (PK) | gain, freq, Q |
| Band 2 (PK) | gain, freq, Q |
| Band 3 (PK) | gain, freq, Q |
| High shelf (HSC) | gain (dB), freq (Hz) |

**⚠ CRITICAL DATA STATUS (see DECISION_LOG D-002):** the CSV currently in
`EmbeddingTestIsolated/data/` is a 150-row **synthetic mock** generated by
`preprocess_safe.py`'s fallback path. It must not be used for any reported result.
Stage 0 of the collection plan is acquiring the real SAFE-DB. All Ns, tag counts,
and floors below are **provisional until the Stage 1 data audit** writes receipts to
`results/stats/`. No number from this document may be cited in a manuscript until it
traces to a Stage 1+ receipt.

**Descriptor floor:** only descriptors with ≥ 20 usable entries after cleaning enter
the main analysis (provisional; finalized at audit). Dropped descriptors are listed
in an appendix table — dropped, not hidden.

**Cleaning (train-split statistics only, see §5):** parse-valid rows; |gain| ≤ 15 dB;
freq > 0; per-descriptor outlier rejection (entries deviating > 2 SD from the
descriptor mean on any band gain). Deduplication by submission hash and near-exact
parameter duplicates from the same IP.

## 4. Arms

| Arm | Name | Mechanism | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Retrieval (grounded) | Per-descriptor centroid computed from TRAIN entries only. For the unseen-descriptor condition: embed descriptor text, retrieve top-k nearest known-descriptor centroids by cosine, similarity-weighted interpolation. | descriptor |
| **B** | LLM zero-shot | LLM prompted with descriptor (+ same metadata fields a SAFE user saw: instrument, genre) → outputs 13-param curve as JSON. No SAFE data in context. | descriptor, instrument, genre |
| **C** | RAG hybrid | Same prompt as B + n=5 TRAIN exemplar curves for that descriptor in context. LLM generates. | descriptor, instrument, genre, exemplars |
| **A′** | Deployed-system honesty arm (secondary) | The keyword-overlap weighting actually shipped in `embed_song_predictor.py` (bag-of-words intersection, not embeddings). Reported so the paper describes the deployed system truthfully. | descriptor |
| **D** | Fine-tuned small LLM | Open-weights 1–3B model, LoRA fine-tuned on TRAIN (descriptor + metadata → 13-param JSON). Trained locally (RTX 5060 Ti); 3 seeds, dev slice held out of TRAIN for early stopping. | descriptor, instrument, genre |
| **E** | Classic-ML floor | Frozen sentence-embedding of descriptor (+ metadata) → small MLP regression head trained on TRAIN. Answers "why an LLM at all for 13-number regression?" — and at ~MB scale it is the shippable product candidate. | descriptor, instrument, genre |
| **F** | RAFT | Same base model as D, LoRA fine-tuned *with* retrieved TRAIN exemplars + distractors in context (retrieval-augmented fine-tuning); evaluated with retrieval at inference. Same viability gate as D — D and F demote together or not at all. | descriptor, instrument, genre, exemplars |

Arms D and E complete the canonical **zero-shot vs. retrieval vs. fine-tune**
triangle (DECISION_LOG D-013), with objective human gold — which is rarer than any
individual arm. Arm F (DECISION_LOG D-016) upgrades the comparison to a **2×2
factorial** — training {none, fine-tuned} × retrieval {none, in-context}: B, C,
D, F occupy the four cells, making main effects and the interaction of the two
grounding mechanisms estimable rather than merely pairwise-comparable. **Viability gate:** if the Stage 1 audit shows usable TRAIN N below
the pre-declared floor (< 1,000 entries total or < 15 descriptors above floor),
Arm D is demoted to exploratory-only before freeze, logged, and excluded from
confirmatory FDR-corrected contrasts.

Honesty note (DECISION_LOG D-003): the deployed EqualizerAI system uses keyword
overlap, not embedding cosine. The paper implements Arm A as true embedding
retrieval and reports A′ separately. We do not describe A′ as embedding retrieval
anywhere.

**Sampling protocol (Arms B, C):** per descriptor × model: 1 greedy sample
(temperature 0, the point estimate) + 10 samples at temperature 0.7 (the
distribution estimate). Sample counts final at freeze.

**Models (Arm B/C):** 4 architecturally distinct families via OpenRouter
(provisional slate: GPT-5.x, Claude Opus 4.x, Gemini 2.x/3.x, DeepSeek V3.x —
pinned model IDs recorded at freeze in DECISION_LOG). No LLM judge exists anywhere
in this pipeline — gold is objective human data — so judge-circularity is
structurally absent. Self-family (Claude assisting analysis) is not a validity
threat for the same reason, but is disclosed in methods.

## 5. Splits — contamination control

- **Main split:** per-descriptor stratified 80/20 train/test at the *entry* level.
  Fixed seed (42), split script + seed committed, resulting eval set hash-locked:
  `data/splits/eval_set.jsonl` + `eval_set.sha256`.
- **All corpus-side statistics** — cleaning thresholds fitted per descriptor,
  outlier rejection, centroids, PCA basis, covariance shrinkage (§6) — are computed
  on TRAIN only. Test entries are touched exactly once, by the final metric scripts.
- **Unseen-descriptor split (secondary):** hold out entire descriptors (sampled
  across frequency strata) to test Arm A's embedding-retrieval generalization and
  Arm B on descriptors that cannot be centroid-matched.
- **Exemplars for Arm C** are drawn from TRAIN only.

## 6. Metrics

### 6.1 Space: rendered frequency response, not raw parameters

Raw parameter vectors are degenerate — different (freq, gain, Q) settings can
produce near-identical transfer functions. All primary metrics are computed on the
**rendered magnitude response**: each 13-param curve → cascade of RBJ-cookbook
biquads (LSC, 3×PK, HSC; shelf Q = 0.71 matching Equalizer APO; fs = 48 kHz) →
|H(f)| in dB on a 256-point log-spaced grid, 20 Hz–20 kHz.
The rendering module gets unit tests against analytically known filter responses
before any result is computed.

- **Primary distance:** RMSE in dB between two rendered curves on the grid
  (log-spectral distance).
- **Robustness variant:** ERB/Bark-weighted version of the same distance (perceptual
  weighting), reported as sensitivity analysis, not primary.

### 6.2 Centrality (per prediction)

D(p, t) = median log-spectral distance from prediction p to the held-out human
curves of descriptor t.

### 6.3 Human-Relative Percentile (HRP) — the mean-artifact control

Raw distance-to-gold structurally favors the centroid arm: a per-descriptor mean
minimizes expected squared distance to samples from that distribution *by
construction*. Reporting raw RMSE alone would manufacture Arm A's victory.

Control: for each held-out human entry h_i, compute D(h_i, t) against the *other*
held-out entries (leave-one-out). This yields the distribution of human-to-human
centrality — how close a typical human is to the human cloud. A prediction's **HRP**
is the percentile of its D within that human distribution. HRP ≈ 50 → the prediction
is as central as a typical human; HRP > 95 → outside the human consensus range.

Interpretation discipline (DECISION_LOG D-005): Arm A is *expected* to sit at HRP ≈ 0
— it is the consensus anchor, an upper bound on centrality, not a fair contestant on
this metric. The claim-bearing contrasts on HRP are B vs. human range, C vs. B, and
family vs. family. Arm A's centrality is reported but never headlined as "beating"
anything.

### 6.4 Distribution coverage (where Arm A can lose)

Human "warm" is a distribution; a centroid is a point mass. For each descriptor ×
arm with sampled outputs: **energy distance** between the sampled curve cloud
(rendered space) and the held-out human cloud. Secondary: variance-ratio
(model cloud variance / human cloud variance) as an over/under-dispersion
diagnostic. This is the metric where H5 gives the LLM arms a genuine path to win,
which keeps the design falsifiable in both directions.

### 6.5 Direction vs. magnitude decomposition (H3)

Per band: sign agreement between predicted gain and TRAIN-consensus human gain sign
(derived binary, reported alongside), and |gain error| conditional on sign agreement
(continuous, primary). Binary is a derived threshold on the continuous value, never
the only recorded value.

### 6.6 In-distribution score (secondary)

PCA basis fitted on TRAIN rendered curves (components to 95% variance);
per-descriptor mean + Ledoit-Wolf-shrunk covariance in PC space; Mahalanobis
distance of each prediction; reported as percentile.

## 7. Statistical analysis plan

- Unit of paired analysis: descriptor. Arms compared with **Wilcoxon signed-rank**
  across descriptors (primary, distribution-free) and a **mixed-effects model**
  (metric ~ arm + (1|descriptor) + (1|model where applicable)) as convergent
  evidence.
- Effect sizes: Cliff's delta for pairwise arm contrasts; bootstrap 95% CIs
  (10,000 resamples) on all headline quantities.
- **Multiple comparisons:** Benjamini-Hochberg FDR at α = 0.05 across the full
  test battery. Full table reported — raw p, adjusted q, survives Y/N — for every
  test run, including the ones that die. Expected-null robustness checks surviving
  as null are reported as evidence of pipeline validity.
- Power note: number of descriptors above floor (known at Stage 1) is the effective
  N for arm contrasts. If < 15 descriptors survive, paired nonparametrics are
  underpowered; fallback pre-declared: entry-level mixed-effects as primary instead
  of descriptor-level Wilcoxon. Decision auto-triggers at audit, logged.

## 8. Threats to validity (pre-registered responses)

1. **Mean artifact** → HRP normalization + distribution-coverage metrics + framing
   discipline (§6.3–6.4).
2. **Split contamination** → train-only statistics; hash-locked eval set; test
   touched once (§5).
3. **LLM memorization of SAFE-DB** — SAFE-DB has been public since 2014 and may be
   in training corpora. Direction of bias: memorization helps the LLM arms, so it is
   *conservative* against H1 ("LLMs fall outside human range") and against the
   retrieval-wins narrative. Robustness: memorization probe — prompt models to
   reproduce SAFE-DB rows/statistics verbatim; report recall evidence either way.
4. **Parameter-space degeneracy** → rendered-response metrics (§6.1).
5. **Noisy gold / human disagreement** → gold operationalized as distribution, not
   point; HRP is defined relative to human self-consistency; per-descriptor human
   variance reported.
6. **Prompt sensitivity** → 3 frozen prompt variants (minimal / persona / few-shot
   format demo without curve content) run as sensitivity battery; prompts
   byte-frozen at Stage 2; any post-freeze wording change = logged protocol change.
7. **Sparse descriptors** → floor + dropped-descriptor appendix (§3).
8. **API nondeterminism / failures** → ≥ 2 successful samples required per cell for
   inclusion; successful-call rate and any truncation reported honestly; retries
   with backoff; raw responses archived as receipts.
9. **Fine-tune overfitting / small-N (Arm D)** → dev slice held out of TRAIN for
   early stopping; 3 seeds with across-seed variance reported; viability gate at
   audit (§4) demotes Arm D to exploratory if data is insufficient; hyperparameters
   frozen at Stage 2 and logged.
10. **Compute-class asymmetry (D/E vs. B)** → not a confound but the *object of
    study* under H6: the arms deliberately span ~3 orders of magnitude of model
    scale with and without access to human gold. Framed explicitly as
    access-vs-scale; parameter counts and training cost reported per arm.

## 9. Scope boundaries

**In scope:** descriptor (+ instrument/genre metadata) → 5-band parametric curve.
**Out of scope:** the live Spotify pipeline (track → tags → descriptor mapping),
Last.fm tag harvesting, EqualizerAPO deployment, listening-quality claims. The
deployed system appears only as honesty arm A′ and in the systems-context section.
No claim about "sounds better" is made without a listening test, which is a
follow-up study, not this paper.
